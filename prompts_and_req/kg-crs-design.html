The Challenge: Why LLMs Alone Fail in E-Commerce

Relying on generic Large Language Models (LLMs) for conversational recommendations introduces critical failures. They lack domain-specific context, leading to user frustration and lost revenue. We face two primary challenges.

1. Item Space Discrepancy

LLMs, trained on general web data, frequently recommend products that are not in the target e-commerce catalog. This creates a "dead-end" for the user, as the recommendation is unusable.

2. Item Information Negligence

LLMs are unaware of crucial interdependencies, such as collaborative patterns (what users *actually* buy together), sequential purchases, or specific, real-time attributes (e.g., inventory, price), operating only on the dialogue context.

The Solution: The Knowledge Graph as "Domain Expert"

We propose an architecture where a Knowledge Graph (KG) serves as the central "domain expert" and "memory" for the CRS. This KG grounds the LLM in factual, domain-specific data, ensuring recommendations are accurate, relevant, and explainable.

Phase 2: Advanced AI Integration

With the KG foundation, we unlock three powerful AI capabilities that directly solve our initial challenges. This is how the KG transitions from a data store to a reasoning engine.

Goal 1: GNN Embeddings for Cold-Start

Problem: How to recommend items to new users (cold-start)?
Solution: We use Relational-GCN (R-GCN) on the graph to generate vector embeddings for all users and products. These embeddings capture complex patterns, allowing us to find similar users based on graph structure, not just shared purchases. This reveals distinct user clusters for better personalization.

Chart 4: User Clusters Identified by GNN Embeddings

Goal 2: KG-LLM Fusion (MIM)

Problem: The "semantic gap." A user's query ("I need a durable laptop") exists in *language space*, while our data ("Feature: 'Durability'") exists in *graph space*.
Solution: We use Mutual Information Maximization (MIM) to create a shared embedding space. By aligning the LLM's text embedding of a review with the GNN's graph embedding of that same review node, the system learns that the *concept* of "durable" is semantically close to the *entity* "Durability".

Chart 5: Semantic Space Alignment Before & After MIM

Goal 3: GraphRAG & Path Reasoning

Problem: LLMs hallucinate recommendations and cannot explain *why* an item is suggested.
Solution: We use GraphRAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). Instead of retrieving simple text, we retrieve a *path* from the KG that answers the user's query. This path provides verifiable, explainable context that grounds the LLM in facts from our catalog.
